My innocent mind knew little that the people, whom I directed to the manager, were policemen in ordinary clothes and were there to talk about me. During those days, I put a business partnership proposal to a young man (my son's friend). He clearly stated that he did like the proposal but was scared that I was under surveillance and having a business relation with me might endanger his own reputation.

After I lost the job at Lynk, at end of 1997, we bought a house and moved to Kellyville (Benalla Avenue) and then started my job hunt again. I got a job in a small plumbing pipes company on Sunnyholt road Blacktown area. I hadn't worked there for long when a person (in ordinary clothes) came and spoke to the manager clearly pointing to me. Don't know if he was a police or a Civil, The next day, the manager called me and fired me from the job. I was clueless and hopeless. I have no words to explain the agony I was going through those days. I got repeatedly sacked from one job after another despite my honest and loyal work ethics. I could neither express this to my friends nor my family. I didn't have an answer to the questions in their eyes.
